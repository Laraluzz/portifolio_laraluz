Defining the project

The Tome is a iOS and Android responsive app that helps D&D players in their playthrough and character building. Most of new and old players have a time-consuming process to create a new character in paper so the application have all the resources so the process is faster and fun. The app also have an AI chat to answer players doubts about the game.

The Problem

Dungeons & Dragons is one of the oldest RPG games and itÂ´s very popular among young and middle-aged people. The game in short is a Dungeon Master (one player) that describes the world around the characters present, who would be other players , who have vast freedom within this world. Since its launch, the game has had 5 editions with different rules and new role-play possibilities. Although fun, creating a playable character within this universe can be complex and time-consuming for both new and old players.

The Solution

New players may feel lost and overwhelmed by the amount of content they have to learn and the application seeks to help by taking the character creation process step by step. Furthermore, players who are already familiar can also use the application to save time and build a character more dynamically and be able to find all the information they need in one place. Also, the player can use the necessary tools during the game to act more quickly and see all the information about his character.

Competitive Analysis

D&D Beyond

D&D Beyond is the official Dungeons & Dragons tool and has a good structure for character creation and subsequent viewing of the player's record. It is possible to roll dice, monitor the use of spells and control the character's life and rest, in addition to being able to see all of their characteristics. Its UI greatly inspired the design of The Tome and the step-by-step process for creating a character. Its only problem is that it does not include all the possibilities of race and class, having only the most basic ones. If the user wants to have more options, they will have to pay a very high price.

PrismScroll

PrismScroll has a good and simple UI based on emojis to represent certain situations and characters and leaves the user with his knowledge about the D&D rules to decide the main numbers of his character such as hit points or AC (Armor Class). So basically the user has an empty sheet to fill out and the app only helps decide basic features like race and class.

Wireframes

The wireframes were created for Android and iOS, so I thought a lot about how to responsibly adapt the design to both platforms and maintain a unique design.

Style Guide

For the style of the app, I challenged myself by using black as a base and contrasting it with white and colors that were neon but still readable. My main focus was the icons I created based in other icons in a sharper style and the illustrations representing races, classes, and backgrounds. For the Typography, I focused on a modern yet medieval look for the headers and a simple and compact font for the text since some parts of the app have a lot of text in small spaces

Takeaways

At the beginning of the project I thought about making an application that would help beginners in D&D but then I realized that it was also important for those who already play to have a quick tool. That's why I created the application intuitively and notifications appear as support, but can also be ignored. There were many changes to the icons is based on the feedback I learned to use icons that made more sense. I learned to create icons with equal sizes and within a style that continued into the illustrations. Adapting the style within the rules of each system was challenging and I learned a lot from the process. I brought a simple but fun app for users to use while playing.
