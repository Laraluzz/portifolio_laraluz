Context

A client wants to resolve the user's problem with their relationship with money. They came with all the UX research and want a responsible app that users can trust their information and money in.

Objective

Create a money management app to help users look at their finances better and make healthier decisions with their money, and also open the possibilities for the user to start saving or investing.

Problem

Spending money by card or cash can make the person oblivious to their actual situation, and step back and see their main financial problems is overwhelming.

Solution

Make a UI design that guides the user through their finance information in a simple flow. The content have to be clear and not stress with the user because of confusing organization.

User Stories

I started analyzing the research and material given by the course and looking for the main problems users were dealing with. The main problem to resolve was to facilitate how the users were recording their information and how to make them see their information in a way they can understand whether what they are doing is bad or good for their finances.

User Flow

I defined the main user stories and created the user flow to guide my design decisions. The user flow was created based on 3 main pages: categorize outlays and see the main information in one place, see and plan their expenses & receipts, be able to save & invest

Low & Mid Fidelity Wireframes

This process helped me start my designs with a quick sketch to see how the pages were going to work. Then I refined these ideas in Figma to start the testing process.

User Testing

The testing was made with 3 people around 20-27 years old. The final design was hugely changed based on their feedback since there were problems with the type of graphs I used and their hierarchy that needed to be adjusted. Also, there was a problem with interaction, where some of them got confused about whether it was clickable or not.

High Fidelity Wireframes

In the high-fidelity wireframes, I corrected the issues and then created additional pages outlined in the user flow, ensuring they were responsive for desktop size.

Brand Design

Defining the brand design is one of the most important parts since all my UI designs cannot be as well effective if the colors are too distracting or the icons too confusing.

The brand's main goal is to help users relate to their finances, which is why it's called "down to earth" to emphasize this idea. At the same time, the app cannot scare the user with a bunch of information, so the main goal is to bring tranquility and confidence to the user by showing each piece of information in the right place, and the colors also help with this hierarchy. The brand identity features a contrast of black and white colors for the main texts, icons, and space divisions, complemented by a pastel purple palette for details such as graphics and buttons. The iconography was made thinking about the overlay of shapes so it is cohesive with the logo design and brings a uniqueness to the app aesthetic that is very delicate and minimalistic.
